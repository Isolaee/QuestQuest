
# Unit Design Guidelines

## Unit Tiers

### Level 1 Units
- **HP:** 12–20
- **Attacks:** 1–2
- **Abilities:** Rarely have any abilities
- **Average Damage:** 3 (per hit), usually attack twice

### Level 2 Units
- **HP:** 25–45
- **Attacks:** 2–4
- **Abilities:** Sometimes have an ability
- **Average Damage:** 5 (per hit), usually attack twice

### Level 3 Units
- **HP:** 50–90
- **Attacks:** 3 or more
- **Abilities:** Often have an ability
- **Average Damage:** 8 (per hit), usually attack three times

---

## Terrain & Movement
- **Average Hit Chance:** 60%
- **Movement Cost:**
	- Normal terrain: 1
	- Hard terrain: 2
	- Very hard terrain: 3

---

## Defense
- **Defense Range:** –50% to +50%

---

## Additional Guidelines
- **Unit Progression:**
	- Higher level units should feel significantly stronger and more versatile than lower level units.
	- Abilities should scale in impact and complexity with unit level.
- **Balance:**
	- Ensure that no single unit type dominates all others; each should have strengths and weaknesses.
	- Consider synergy with terrain and abilities.
- **Special Abilities:**
	- Level 1: Rare, simple effects (e.g., minor buffs or debuffs)
	- Level 2: Occasional, moderate effects (e.g., conditional attacks, situational bonuses)
	- Level 3: Frequent, impactful effects (e.g., area attacks, strong passives)
- **Flavor & Theme:**
	- Units should fit the game's world and lore.
	- Consider unique traits or quirks for memorable design.

---

*Use these guidelines as a baseline for designing and balancing units. Adjust as needed for gameplay and thematic fit.*
